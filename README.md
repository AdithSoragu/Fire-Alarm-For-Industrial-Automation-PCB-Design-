# 🧠 Introduction to Fire Alarm Systems

Fire alarm systems are important safety devices used in industries and commercial buildings to detect fire hazards and provide warning alerts.

The system continuously monitors environmental conditions such as:
- Smoke
- Heat
- Fire presence

When abnormal conditions are detected:
- The buzzer activates
- LEDs indicate warning signals
- Nearby personnel are alerted immediately

Industrial environments contain:

Industrial fire alarm systems improve:

# 🔄 Working Principle

The fire alarm system continuously monitors fire conditions using sensing circuitry.

---

## Step 1: Fire Detection

The photodiode detects:
- Light intensity changes
- Fire-related signals

The detected signal is converted into an electrical signal.

---

## Step 2: Comparator Processing

The LM358 comparator compares:
- Sensor voltage
- Reference voltage

If the detected signal exceeds the threshold:
- Output becomes HIGH

---

## Step 3: Transistor Switching

The BC548 transistor acts as a switch and activates:
- Relay module
- Alarm circuit

---

## Step 4: Alarm Activation

Once activated:
- Buzzer turns ON
- LEDs glow
- Warning signal is generated
